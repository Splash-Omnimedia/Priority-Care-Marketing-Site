A meaningful impact. A minimal footprint.

No new clinical workflows. No additional staff. No new systems.

One of the first questions most administrators ask is how much disruption a new program brings. With Priority Care MD, the answer is straightforward. Getting started is as simple as receiving a flag or alert within your existing scheduling and triage screens notifying your personnel of an appointment request — something your IT team can typically integrate very quickly.

When a member requests an appointment, they're identified within your existing workflow. Your scheduling and triage team handles it using the same clinical judgment and processes they already use every day. Nothing changes about how care is delivered. What changes is how efficiently certain patients move through the administrative process of getting there.

The result is a program that improves access and generates additional revenue without adding burden to your team.

A smarter return on your existing resources

Additional revenue, without additional investment.

When a Priority Care MD member is seen within the defined access window, your hospital receives its standard insurance reimbursement plus an administrative stipend from Priority Care MD, paid by the 15th of the following month. You can then determine how the stipend is distributed and utilized within your organization — whether that's directing funds toward outreach programs, operational improvements, or rewarding the physicians who are delivering timely, quality care through the program.

Your existing clinical capacity, your physicians, your clinics, your scheduling infrastructure, generates additional revenue simply by honoring a timely access commitment you already believe in.

For hospital systems with existing employer relationships, Priority Care MD also creates a pathway to route higher-margin, highly motivated patient visits directly into your existing clinics. A force multiplier on relationships you've already built.
