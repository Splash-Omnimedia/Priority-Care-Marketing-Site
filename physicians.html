The reality of modern practice

Burnout isn't a personal failing. It's a structure problem.

Fifteen minutes per patient. A schedule packed so tight there's no room to breathe, let alone think. Cancellations that leave gaps you can't fill fast enough. A backlog that grows faster than you can clear it. And a system that measures your value in relative value units rather than in the outcomes you actually care about.

Burnout is the predictable result of a structure built on volume over value — one never designed to support the people doing the work. The physicians who feel it most are often the ones who care the most. You deserve better than that. Your patients do too.

A structure that works with you

Participation fits within your existing practice — without disrupting it.

Your schedule doesn't change. A common starting point is reserving one appointment slot per day for Priority Care MD members. If no request comes in within 48 hours, that slot returns to your regular schedule — available for a new referral or a patient on your waitlist. Nothing's wasted. You stay in control.

When a member is seen within the defined access window, you receive your standard insurance reimbursement plus an administrative stipend from Priority Care MD — a stipend that often improves your profit by 200–300%, paid monthly. Stipends are earned by meeting the access benchmarks.

Priority access

Stipend paid for visits fulfilled within 72 hours of the request.

Same-day access

Stipend paid for acute visits fulfilled within 24 hours.

Premier access

Additional compensation tier for procedures and diagnostic imaging.

Your clinical autonomy stays completely intact. Priority Care MD facilitates access; every decision about care belongs to you and your patient. Stipends are tied exclusively to scheduling performance benchmarks — never to referrals or patient placement.

Preferred follow-up time slots

Your schedule works for your patients. Priority Care MD makes sure of it.

By reserving your first or last appointment slot of the day for Priority Care MD follow-up patients, you give working adults and parents the ability to be seen without losing a full workday, burning through PTO, or scrambling for childcare. For your patients, it's the difference between getting follow-up care and putting it off. For you, these slots carry their own stipend eligibility.

  • Working adults — seen before or after work. No lost workdays, no burned PTO.
  • Parents — early appointments mean kids are seen and parents are back at their desk before lunch.
  • Stipend eligible — preferred follow-up slots carry their own stipend tier, recognizing the value they deliver.

Better access for your patients. No disruption to your day.

What this looks like in practice

Priority Care MD works across every part of your practice.

Not just new patient visits, but the full spectrum of care your patients need.

New patient priority visits

When a member submits a request that matches your specialty, you receive it within your existing workflow. See them within 72 hours and the stipend is earned. It's that straightforward.

Same-day acute visits

For established patients with urgent needs, same-day access carries its own stipend structure. Getting someone in within 24 hours when it matters most is recognized and compensated accordingly.

Preferred follow-up slots

Early-morning and end-of-day follow-up appointments for working adults and parents are stipend eligible. Your patients protect their workday; you're recognized for making it possible.

Premier Plan procedures & imaging

For procedures requiring sedation or diagnostic imaging, morning scheduling carries an additional compensation tier. A patient who doesn't have to fast all day for a 2 PM procedure shows up prepared, on time, and grateful.

The more of your practice Priority Care MD can support, the more the program works for you.
