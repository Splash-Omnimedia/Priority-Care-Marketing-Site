The hidden cost of waiting

When an employee needs a doctor, the average wait is more than 30 days.

For a specialist, it can stretch to three months or longer. And while they wait, they don't just sit at home. They come to work worried, distracted, and not fully present — sometimes because they can't afford to burn PTO on a six-week wait. Or they don't come in at all.

Absenteeism

Sick employees who can't come in. Work doesn't get done, teams absorb the pressure, and productivity drops.

Presenteeism

Sick employees who show up anyway — at a fraction of capacity, spreading illness to others, and costing much more as other team members get ill.

Both are expensive, largely preventable, and get significantly better when employees can actually get care when they need it.
